The Network

About the Network

Well nice number aren't they ? But where the fudge did they came from ? Well we used both the the Rickipedia and the Rick & Morty API to come up with the last numbers. We took the well formated informations about characters form the API and we crossed them with the content of the wikipedia pages. To create the networks we parsed each Wikipedia page of the characters to find links. When a link was found we created an edge from the character to the other character.

General View

Above you can see the complete character network, we highlighted the Ricks in blue and the Mortys in yellow . We also printed the node size depending on their degree which is why Rick & Morty have such big nodes. Let's dive a little bit deeper into the degree analysis of our network.

Degree Analysis

Well nothing very surprising here, Rick & Morty, as well as their family members, have a big amounts of nodes compared to other characters. Lots of other characters only know a few others, which makes sense considering the structure of the series, which focus on random adventures over episodes.

Centrality

Betweenness Centrality

Since betweenness is a measure of which node has the most shortest paths going through it, a high betweenness centrality means that this node is the character to know, if you want to be able to get as fast as possible from A to B in the network. As expected, Rick and Morty are most central, followed my the remaining members of the Smith family. Jerry is probably third because he joins Rick and Morty on most adventures, compared to the other two family members.

Centrality Eigenvector

The Eigenvector centrality is a measure of how nodes are connected to other nodes with high degree, and in that sense it is a ranking. Characters have high eigenvector centrality probably because of popularity, i.e. they appear often with other high-degree characters.

Again, Morty and Rick appear with highest centrality, whereas Jerry is on fifth place. Perhaps, like explained above with betweenness, because he is on many adventures in different locations, where a lot of characters with low degrees appear. Simply because these characters appear in locations that make them isolated from the rest of the network.

Everything as expected

Well so far we haven't realize anything surprising. But is it a bad sign ? No it's actually a good sign because it shows that the data we collected is in correlation with the show which is a very good things this far !

Communities

This section analyzes the communities by comparing four different algorithms for community detection. Plots are used to show the structure of communities and which of the main characters are central to a specific community. For instance if Jerry Smith is the central character in a given community nodes will be colored green to match Jerry's awesome shirt. Likewise for Rick, where nodes are colored turquoise to match his pointy hair. If Rick and Morty are both in the same community, nodes will be colored in a flashy green to recall the portal gun. For each plot, node sizes are dependent on the size of the communities, such that bigger communities have bigger nodes.

Greedy Communities

Modularity : 0.48

The greedy modularity maximization divides characters into communities decreasing by modularity, i.e. the first community will have the highest modularity. As shown by the graph this actually results in community size decreasing simultaneously with the decreasing modularity. Since communities are based upon maximization of modularity, a few big communities exist. This is due to the high degrees of Rick and Morty. This algorithm, as described by it's name, will divide communities with the highest modularity, since that is inherent in the functionality of the algorithm.

Fluid Communities

Modularity : 0.41

The fluid communities algorithm requires defining k number of communities, since it assigns communities randomly to k nodes and divides communitites by randomly iterating over nodes and assigning them to communities based on which communities the neighbors of the given node are divided into. This randomization results in more distinct communities than some of the other algorithms.

Label Propagation Communities

Modularity : 0.24

The label propagation algorithm performs worst of all the algorithms, when considering modularity. All members of the Smith family are clustered together into one large community that dominates the graph, also visually. This large community exists because of the labelling approach used by the algorithm, where nodes are divided into communities if their neighbors contain similar labels. This approach will enhance communities with high-degree nodes, thus generating this dominant community containing all members of the family.

Louvain Communities

Modularity : 0.45

What is interesting about the Louvain algorithm is that the approach it uses to divide communities is similar to greedy modularity maximization. The major difference is the initial grouping of nodes locally. Perhaps this local grouping is the reason why Rick and Morty reside in separate communities, as compared to the greedy modularity maximization algorithm.
